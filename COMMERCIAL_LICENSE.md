## When you need a commercial license

You need a commercial license if **any** of the following applies:

- You ship Crucible (or a derivative work) as part of a **closed-source product**.
- You expose Crucible's functionality over a network — **SaaS, hosted service,
  internal API, customer-facing dashboard, embedded web service, etc.** — and
  do **not** wish to release the Corresponding Source under
  AGPL-3.0 §13 ("Remote Network Interaction").
- You **embed** Crucible into a system where AGPL-3.0 copyleft conflicts with
  your internal IP, customer contracts, or compliance requirements.
- You require **indemnification, warranty, SLA-backed support, or a written
  guarantee** of any kind.
- You want the right to keep **modifications, plug-ins, or downstream
  integrations proprietary**.

If your usage is **fully AGPL-compliant** — i.e. you publish the complete
source of any Crucible-derived work and any service built on it, including
all modifications, under AGPL-3.0 — you do **not** need a commercial license.

## How to request a commercial license

Email **supervenus928@gmail.com** with the following:

1. **Company / project name** and a one-line description of what you do.
2. **Intended deployment**: internal tool, customer-facing SaaS, on-prem,
   embedded device, OEM, etc.
3. **Approximate scale**: user count, request volume, or revenue tier.
4. **What you need**, e.g.:
   - source-rights / proprietary-derivative permission only
   - priority support / SLA
   - indemnification
   - custom feature work or roadmap influence
5. Any **timeline constraints** (procurement deadlines, launch dates, etc.).

You will receive proposed terms and pricing within a reasonable time frame.

## What a commercial license typically grants

Commercial terms are agreed **per engagement** in writing. A typical
commercial license includes:

- Permission to use, modify, and distribute Crucible **without** AGPL-3.0
  copyleft obligations (i.e. no requirement to publish derivative source).
- The right to keep your **modifications, integrations, and hosted services
  proprietary**.
- Optional add-ons:
  - Priority technical support and security advisories
  - Custom feature work or roadmap influence
  - Indemnification and warranty

Specific terms (price, scope, term length, support SLA, payment schedule,
governing law) are negotiated and signed per engagement.
